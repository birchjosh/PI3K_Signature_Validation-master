CreateSignatures Report

I. Analysis

  • I ran a signature analysis using a training set of Pi3K_signature_gfp.txt and Pi3K_signature_tpm.txt. I generated predictions on icbp_Rsubread_tpmlog.txt.
  • I used the BinReg 2 algorithm with 400 genes and 1 metagene.
  • I applied DWD normalization.
  • For the statistical (Markov chain Monte Carlo) simulation, I discarded 1,000 samples for the burn-in and then collected 5,000 samples for the model.

II. Results

Figure 1: Signature Heatmap. In this heatmap, each row represents a gene in the signature. The first 8 columns are the samples from the train0 data set, and the remaining 9 columns are the samples from the train1 data set. Warm colors indicate high expression of the gene, and cool colors indicate low expression. Figure 2: Predictions. This scatter plot shows the predictions from the signature for each sample. On the Y-axis, high probabilities indicate that the gene expression profile of the sample better resembles the train 1 class, while low probabilities indicate a closer resemblance to train 0. The blue and red circles are the predictions (from leave-one-out cross-validation) on the train 0 and train 1 samples, respectively. The black squares are the predictions on the test samples. The error bars show the 95% credible interval. The X-axis, the Metagene Score, is the magnitude of the sample on the first principal component. This is used to help separate the samples across the plot.

The raw values from this plot are available as a tab-delimited text file: probabilities.txt.
